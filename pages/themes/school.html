🧒 Why Focus on Youth?

Children are the most powerful agents of behaviour change in any household. When a child learns to segregate waste at school, they come home and correct their parents. When a student plants a tree, they water it every day because they own it. School engagement isn't just feel-good — it's the most cost-effective way to sustain a cleanliness culture beyond the 15 days of the Pakhwada.

CCL's school engagement model combines competition (for energy), creativity (for motivation), and ownership (for sustainability) — making each student a genuine Swachhata Champion in their own home and street.

School Activities

Competitions, quizzes, plays, and pledges — making cleanliness the cool thing to do

🎨

Poster & Painting Competitions

Students creating visual art on themes of cleanliness, anti-plastic, water conservation, and green living — winners get their art displayed at the area office.

❓

Swachhata Quiz

Inter-school quiz competitions testing knowledge of sanitation, hygiene, environment, and the Swachh Bharat Mission — with cash prizes and certificates.

🎭

Nukkad Natak (School Edition)

Student theatre groups performing cleanliness awareness skits at school assemblies and community events — student-written, student-performed.

📝

Essay & Slogan Writing

Students writing on topics like "My Clean Village", "Zero Plastic Future", and "Water is Life" — with the best essays published on the campaign portal.

🌱

One Student, One Tree

Every student plants a sapling during the campaign and commits to its care for a year — generating both green cover and a sense of personal environmental responsibility.

🧹

School Cleanliness Drives

Student-led cleanliness drives covering classrooms, toilets, playgrounds, and surrounding streets — with teachers joining as co-participants, not supervisors.
