💧 Why Water Matters at CCL

Coal mining operations interact closely with local hydrology — through drainage systems, effluent management, and proximity to rivers and ponds. Swachhata Pakhwada 2026 recognises that mining companies have both a heightened responsibility and a unique opportunity to model water stewardship.

From removing floating waste from colony ponds to constructing rainwater harvesting structures, CCL areas are demonstrating that industrial operations and clean water can coexist when the right systems and consciousness are in place.

Water Activities This Pakhwada

Conservation and cleanliness go hand in hand — every drop protected is a future secured

🌊

Pond & Lake Cleaning Drives

Removing floating garbage, hyacinth overgrowth, and silt from colony ponds and nearby water bodies — restoring them for community use.

🌧️

Rainwater Harvesting Structures

Construction and maintenance of rooftop collection systems and percolation pits to recharge groundwater and reduce dependence on bore wells.

🚰

Hand Pump Surrounds Cleaning

Clearing drainage channels around hand pumps and standposts to prevent stagnant water and surface contamination at community water points.

🌿

Stream Bank Restoration

Planting grass and shrubs along stream banks near mine perimeters to stabilise soil, reduce erosion, and prevent debris runoff into watercourses.

📊

Water Quality Awareness

Demonstrations using simple test kits showing communities how to identify turbid or contaminated water — and what to do before using it for drinking.

🚫

Open Drain & Nullah Clearing

Desilting and clearing of drains and nullahs to prevent flooding, mosquito breeding, and contamination of drinking water during the monsoon season.
