🚫 CCL's Plastic-Free Commitment

India banned single-use plastics in 2022. Swachhata Pakhwada 2026 is CCL's intensive reinforcement of that ban — making it visible, enforceable, and culturally embedded across all 10 areas. This means plastic-free canteens, no polythene in colonies, and active collection of legacy plastic waste from mine sites and public spaces.

The campaign also distributes reusable cloth bags, steel tiffin carriers, and biodegradable leaf plates as direct substitutes — because people adopt alternatives only when alternatives are in their hands.

Campaign Activities

From collection drives to cultural nudges — every tactic counts

🛍️

Cloth Bag Distribution

Branded jute and cloth bags distributed to workers, colony residents, and schools to replace polythene carry bags in daily use.

🗑️

Plastic Collection Drives

Organised collection of legacy plastic waste from mine sites, roadsides, and water bodies — sorted for responsible disposal or recycling.

🍽️

Plastic-Free Canteens

CCL mine canteens transitioning to steel, ceramic, and leaf-plate service — eliminating disposable cups, plates, and cutlery at the source.

🎨

Anti-Plastic Art Competitions

Students creating posters, sculptures, and art from collected plastic waste — turning the problem into powerful advocacy material.

📢

Nukkad Natak on Plastics

Street theatre performances dramatising the journey of a plastic bag — from a shop to a drain to a cow's stomach — for high emotional impact.

🏆

Plastic-Free Office Challenge

Areas competing to be certified plastic-free — offices are inspected and the cleanest area receives recognition at the campaign closing ceremony.
