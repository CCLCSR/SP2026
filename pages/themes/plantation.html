🌿 Why Plantation in a Cleanliness Drive?

Swachhata is not just about removing dirt — it is about restoring the environment. Trees stabilise mine overburden dumps, prevent soil erosion, filter dust from mining operations, and provide shade in residential colonies. Plantation is cleanliness in its deepest ecological sense.

CCL's plantation drives during SP 2026 prioritise native Jharkhand species with high survival rates — Sal, Arjun, Mahua, Neem, and Bamboo — over ornamental species, ensuring each sapling serves the local ecosystem rather than just looking good on paper.

Plantation Activities

From nursery to mine dump — green cover goes where it is needed most

🌱

Mass Plantation Drives

Large-scale plantation events at mine peripheries, colony parks, schools, and roadsides — with officers, workers, and students planting together.

⛰️

Overburden Dump Greening

Stabilising mine waste dumps with pioneer grass species and pioneer trees — converting an environmental liability into a green asset.

🏫

School & Eco-Club Plantation

Each student in CCL schools planting one sapling and taking ownership of its survival — building environmental stewardship from a young age.

🌾

Medicinal & Fruit Tree Planting

Planting neem, amla, guava, drumstick, and other useful trees in colony areas — combining green cover with community nutrition and health benefits.

🌊

Riparian Buffer Planting

Establishing trees and shrubs along stream banks and water body margins to prevent erosion and filter runoff from mine sites.

🎋

Bamboo Plantation

Bamboo groves planted at mine peripheries and colony boundaries — fast-growing, carbon-sequestering, and commercially valuable for communities.
