🙌 What is Shramdaan?

Shramdaan — voluntary donation of physical labour — is one of Gandhian principles at the heart of Swachhata Pakhwada. Rather than contracting out cleanliness, CCL officers, workers, and community members roll up their sleeves and clean their own spaces together.

This collective ownership approach ensures that people feel responsible for the cleanliness of their environment — not just during the Pakhwada, but throughout the year. When leaders participate alongside workers, it sends an unmistakable message: sanitation is everyone's dignity.

Types of Cleanliness Drives

From plogging runs to hospital cleaning — every format serves a different community need

🏃

Plogging Drives

Running while picking up litter — combining fitness with environmental action. Organised across residential areas, parks, and mine perimeters.

🏥

Hospital & Dispensary Cleaning

Special drives at CCL health facilities ensuring that medical premises maintain the highest hygiene standards for patients and staff.

🏫

School Premises Cleaning

Students, teachers, and parents working together to clean school grounds, toilets, and surrounding areas — instilling habits young.

🌳

Parks & Public Space Drives

Community-led cleaning of parks, playgrounds, and open spaces in CCL colonies — reclaiming public spaces for healthy recreation.

🛣️

Road & Colony Cleaning

Systematic cleaning of colony streets, drains, and common areas with participation from resident welfare associations.

⛏️

Mine Site Housekeeping

Organised cleanliness drives at mine offices, workshops, and operational areas — demonstrating that workplace hygiene is non-negotiable.
