🗑️ Four-Stream Waste Segregation

🟢

Green — Wet / Organic

Kitchen waste, food scraps, vegetable peels, garden trimmings. Goes to composting.

🔵

Blue — Dry / Recyclable

Paper, cardboard, glass, metals, hard plastics. Goes to recycling aggregators.

🔴

Red — Hazardous

Batteries, e-waste, chemicals, medical waste. Handled by authorised agencies only.

⚫

Black — Reject / Inert

Soiled items, contaminated waste not suitable for recycling or composting.

🔄 Kabad se Kanchan — Waste to Wealth

The "Kabad se Kanchan" (Scrap to Gold) initiative trains Self-Help Groups to convert waste materials into products of economic value — bags from old newspapers, decorations from plastic bottles, compost from organic waste, and handicrafts from scrap metal.

This circular economy approach creates livelihoods while solving a waste problem — turning the cost of disposal into a source of income for women's groups in CCL colonies. Five demonstration units are being set up across areas during SP 2026.

SWM Activities This Pakhwada

Building systems, demonstrating alternatives, and rewarding good practice

🗂️

Waste Audit Drives

Teams quantifying the type and volume of waste generated at mine sites and colonies — establishing a baseline for year-round improvement.

🌱

Composting Demonstrations

Setting up and demonstrating vermicomposting pits and aerobic composting at schools, hospitals, and colony common areas.

🛠️

Bin Installation Drives

Installing colour-coded segregation bins at canteens, offices, schools, and public spaces to make correct disposal effortless.

🧵

Waste-to-Art Workshops

SHGs converting collected dry waste into saleable products — bags, decorations, plant pots, and craft items with market value.

🏆

Cleanest Workplace Award

Areas competing for the SP 2026 cleanest workplace certification — judged on waste segregation, bin usage, and zero open dumping.

📋

Waste Collection Records

Daily records of waste collected, segregated, and disposed/recycled — submitted as part of the campaign report to Coal India.
