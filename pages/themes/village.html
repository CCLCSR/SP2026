🏛️ Gram Sabha Engagement

Gram Sabhas — village assemblies — are the most democratic forum for community decision-making in rural India. CCL's Kuju area pioneered the use of Gram Sabha meetings during SP 2026 to bring sanitation into the village's own governance agenda, ensuring local leaders take ownership of cleanliness outcomes.

By engaging panchayat leaders, sarpanches, and ward members directly, these sessions embed sanitation goals into local plans that outlast the Pakhwada fortnight and continue throughout the year.

Community Initiatives

Meeting communities where they are — in haats, at wells, and at the village gate

🏛️

Gram Sabha Meetings

Village assemblies where CCL and panchayat leaders together pass resolutions on open defecation, waste disposal, and water hygiene.

🏪

Haat Outreach (Weekly Markets)

IEC materials and awareness demonstrations at weekly village markets — reaching thousands of community members at a single touch point.

🚪

Door-to-Door Campaigns

Teams visiting each household in surrounding villages to explain toilet use, waste segregation, and clean water storage practices.

💧

Village Water Point Cleaning

Clearing the surroundings of hand pumps, wells, and piped water points to prevent contamination and promote hygienic water use.

🌾

Field & Common Land Drives

Collective cleaning of community land, pathways, and agricultural peripheries to demonstrate that public spaces are shared responsibility.

👩

Mahila Mandal Mobilisation

Women's groups playing a leading role in organising community clean-up events, household hygiene surveys, and awareness sessions.
